# Omni-Scriptr Analytics & Reporting Strategy

This document outlines the strategy for measuring, analyzing, and reporting on Omni-Scriptr's social media performance. A data-driven approach is essential to optimize our strategy, maximize ROI, and ensure our efforts align with business objectives.

## 1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Our KPIs are divided into four categories, mapping directly to our high-level goals.

### 1.1. Awareness

*   **Metric:** Follower Growth
    *   **Description:** The net increase in followers on each platform.
    *   **Benchmark:** Grow total audience by 15% quarter-over-quarter.
*   **Metric:** Impressions & Reach
    *   **Description:** The total number of times our content is displayed (impressions) and the number of unique people who see it (reach).
    *   **Benchmark:** Increase reach by 20% month-over-month.

### 1.2. Engagement

*   **Metric:** Engagement Rate
    *   **Description:** (Likes + Comments + Shares) / Total Followers.
    *   **Benchmark:** Maintain an average engagement rate of 3-5% across all platforms.
*   **Metric:** Applause Rate (Likes), Conversation Rate (Comments), Amplification Rate (Shares)
    *   **Description:** A breakdown of engagement to understand *how* people are interacting with our content.
    *   **Benchmark:** Track shifts in these rates to identify content that sparks conversation and sharing.

### 1.3. Conversion

*   **Metric:** Click-Through Rate (CTR)
    *   **Description:** The percentage of people who click the link in our posts.
    *   **Benchmark:** Achieve an average CTR of 2% on posts with links.
*   **Metric:** Social-Referred Traffic
    *   **Description:** The number of website sessions originating from social media channels.
    *   **Benchmark:** Increase social-referred traffic by 10% month-over-month.
*   **Metric:** Social Conversion Rate
    *   **Description:** The percentage of social-referred visitors who sign up for a free trial.
    *   **Benchmark:** Achieve a 5% conversion rate from social traffic.
*   **Metric:** Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
    *   **Description:** The total cost of a paid campaign divided by the number of new customers acquired.
    *   **Benchmark:** Keep CPA below 50% of the customer's first-year lifetime value (LTV).

## 2. Tools & Technology

*   **Social Media Management Platform (e.g., Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social):**
    *   **Use:** To schedule posts and access built-in analytics for tracking platform-specific metrics like follower growth, impressions, and engagement rates.
*   **Google Analytics 4 (GA4):**
    *   **Use:** To track website-level metrics. We will use UTM parameters on all social media links to accurately attribute traffic and conversions.
    *   **UTM Structure:** `utm_source=[platform]`, `utm_medium=social`, `utm_campaign=[campaign_name]`, `utm_content=[post_identifier]`
*   **Native Platform Analytics (Facebook Ads Manager, LinkedIn Campaign Manager):**
    *   **Use:** To get granular data on paid campaign performance, including CPM, CPC, and ROAS.

## 3. Reporting & Cadence

### 3.1. Weekly "Pulse Check"

*   **Audience:** Social Media Manager.
*   **Format:** A brief, informal review of the past week's performance.
*   **Content:**
    *   Top-performing and worst-performing posts.
    *   Any significant spikes or dips in engagement.
    *   Review of paid campaign performance (pacing, initial results).
*   **Purpose:** To quickly identify what's working and make minor adjustments to the content schedule or ad spend.

### 3.2. Monthly Performance Review

*   **Audience:** Marketing Team.
*   **Format:** A detailed dashboard/report.
*   **Content:**
    *   Month-over-month and year-over-year tracking of all primary KPIs.
    *   Analysis of content pillars: Which themes are resonating most?
    *   Funnel analysis: How are our paid campaigns performing at each stage?
    *   Key learnings and actionable insights.
*   **Purpose:** To provide a comprehensive overview of performance against goals and inform the upcoming month's content and campaign strategy.

### 3.3. Quarterly Strategy Review

*   **Audience:** Company Leadership.
*   **Format:** A formal presentation.
*   **Content:**
    *   High-level summary of performance against quarterly objectives.
    *   Contribution of social media to business goals (e.g., leads, sign-ups).
    *   Analysis of ROI for paid campaigns and influencer marketing.
    *   Strategic recommendations for the next quarter.
*   **Purpose:** To demonstrate the business impact of social media and secure buy-in for future initiatives and budget.

By consistently tracking these metrics and maintaining a regular reporting cadence, we will ensure our social media strategy remains agile, effective, and aligned with Omni-Scriptr's growth objectives.
